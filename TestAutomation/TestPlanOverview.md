\# 3" Midrange Driver Comparison Test Plan



\## Drivers

\- Tang Band W3-2141

\- Beyma 3FR30V2

\- Eminence Alpha 3-8

\- Dayton RS100-4

\- Vifa NE123W-08



\---



\## Test Fixture



\- 2 ft × 2 ft test baffle

\- Replaceable driver mount inserts

\- Same sealed/stuffed rear chamber style for each driver

\- No leaks, rattles, or loose wiring

\- Keep first reflection at least \~4 ft away if possible



\---



\## Mic Setup



\- Mic distance: \*\*2 ft\*\*

\- Mic fixed on driver axis

\- Same mic/preamp/interface gain for all tests

\- Use preamp lights: solid green, maybe touching yellow, never red



Reference conversion:



\- \*\*95 dB @ 1 m ≈ 99.3 dB @ 2 ft\*\*



\---



\## Level Matching



Goal: equalize acoustic workload in the intended mid band.



1\. Pick one reference driver.

2\. Estimate voltage for \~95 dB @ 1 m equivalent.

3\. Play \*\*300 Hz – 2 kHz band-limited pink noise\*\*.

4\. Measure mic RMS level at 2 ft.

5\. Save that mic level as the reference.

6\. For every other driver, adjust amp output until the same 300 Hz–2 kHz mic RMS level is reached.

7\. Do not change mic/preamp/interface gain after this.



\---



\## 1. Frequency Response



Measure wider than the intended passband.



Suggested range:



\- \*\*150 Hz – 8/10 kHz\*\*



Purpose:



\- confirm usable response through intended band

\- check response past crossover points

\- identify breakup peaks

\- identify peaks needing notch/mitigation

\- judge crossover friendliness



Do not over-optimize final voicing from this test baffle.



\---



\## 2. THD Sweep



Sweep:



\- \*\*8 kHz down to 150 Hz\*\*



Run at several matched levels:



\- moderate

\- loud

\- target/max level



Watch especially:



\- 250–500 Hz distortion rise

\- 2nd vs 3rd harmonic behavior

\- sudden distortion jumps



\---



\## 3. Two-Tone IMD



Suggested pairs:



\- 250 Hz + 1 kHz

\- 300 Hz + 1 kHz

\- 400 Hz + 1 kHz

\- 250 Hz + 1.5 kHz

\- 300 Hz + 1.5 kHz

\- 400 Hz + 1.5 kHz



Watch for:



\- sidebands around upper tone

\- extra spectral grass

\- rapid worsening as LF tone drops



\---



\## 4. Dense Multitone



Run same total RMS level for each band:



\- 250 Hz – 2 kHz

\- 300 Hz – 2 kHz

\- 350 Hz – 2 kHz

\- 400 Hz – 2 kHz



Watch for:



\- broadband hash

\- filled-in space between tones

\- loss of cleanliness when lower limit drops



\---



\## 5. Compression / Headroom Check



Optional but useful.



Run a short stepped-level test using the same 300 Hz – 2 kHz band-limited noise.



Look for:



\- output failing to rise linearly with input

\- tonal balance changing with level

\- distortion rising disproportionately



This is mainly a high-SPL/headroom check, not the primary ranking factor.



\---



\## Ranking Priorities



1\. Usable frequency response through and beyond intended passband

2\. Crossover integration friendliness

3\. IMD vs lower crossover point

4\. THD rise from 500 Hz down to 250 Hz

5\. Compression/headroom at high SPL

6\. Practical fit, cost, and availability



\---



\## Final Goal



Determine which driver best covers:



> \*\*\~300–400 Hz up to \~2 kHz\*\*



while retaining enough margin to experiment down toward:



> \*\*250 Hz\*\*
